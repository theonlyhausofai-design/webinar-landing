Phase 01 · Find your niche
01

Discovery interview

Seven sections of structured self-interview. What you have done before, who you talk to easily, what kind of work you actually enjoy. Anchors every downstream decision.

02

Score five niche candidates

You name five niches. The agent scores each on seven factors: ticket size, urgency, online maturity, your unfair advantage, saturation, two more. Top three flagged.

/score-niches
~30 min
Free
03

Deep research on the top three

Apify scrapes Google Business Profile, Facebook, agency portfolios, organic SERP winners, social discussion. Seven sub-tasks per niche. A structured intelligence pack you read before deciding.

/research
3 to 5 hrs

04

Pick the niche

You commit. The state machine records the niche. Nothing was tailored before this. Everything that follows is tailored.

/pick-niche
~15 min
Free
Phase 02 · Tailor the system
05

Build your niche template

Apify captures 8 to 12 top-of-niche sites. Claude Vision scores each on an 8-category conversion rubric. You see the top three with screenshots. You pick one or mix. The stack writes a full niche playbook into templates/{your-niche}/niche-playbook/ covering voice grammar, hero composition, photo manifest, trust signals, motion preset, CRO rules. Vite + React scaffold ready to run.

/build-niche-template
60 to 90 min
~$5 Apify
06

Craft your offer and cold DMs

Positioning, stage, irresistible offer, cold-DM scripts. The output is the file you copy into LinkedIn and Instagram from day one. Tuned to your niche, your voice, your numbers.

/craft-offer
~2 hrs
Free
Phase 03 · Ship for a real client
07

Tailor the factory and run a client

You bring a real client. The factory tailors itself to their data, then runs them through the 13-stage pipeline. Every stage has a gate. The brand DNA stage carries an approval gate where you and the client sign off before code is written. Subsequent gates run as automated QA checks.

/tailor-factory /run-factory
1 to 3 days
~$5 / client
The 13 stages, in order, per client
  1. Intake
  2. Research
  3. SEO planning
  4. Asset harvest
  5. Strategy
  6. Copywriting
  7. Brand DNA (approval gate)
  8. Brand resonance (optional)
  9. Hero image (Nano Banana)
  10. Build + QA (build · personalise · uplift · fidelity · SOP · perf)
  11. Deploy
  12. Delivery
  13. Proposal
08

Deploy YOUR content engine

This one is for you, not your client. The content engine is a Next.js app you deploy to your own Vercel. It scrapes your niche's hashtags and creators, surfaces real ideas spiking right now, and writes them in your voice. You post on your own LinkedIn and Instagram. Leads come inbound. The alternative to grinding cold DMs from Module 06.

/generate-ce-brief /deploy-engine /walk-engine
~60 min
Free tier

Each client sharpens the template

Every build produces lessons. A locked phrase that didn't land. A section the niche wants in a different order. A trust badge you missed first time. These feed back into Module 05's niche template so your second client is faster than your first. Tenth client is faster than your fifth.

By the end of the journey

You own six things.

01 · Agency

A productized agency

Not a job. A clear offer with a specific niche, a specific price, and a process you can describe in one sentence.

02 · Template

A niche template

Tuned to your niche by your own research. Improves with every client. Lives in your repo, your fork, your control.

03 · Factory

A 13-stage factory

Tailored to your niche. Every stage gated and reviewable. Builds a real production website end-to-end.

04 · Engine

Your own lead-gen engine

Deployed on your Vercel. Loaded with your niche context. Surfaces real ideas you post on your own social so leads come to you instead of you chasing them.

05 · Client

A first paying client

Two lead channels at once. Cold DMs from Module 06 go out. The content engine from Module 08 pulls in. Most students sign their first client in week three.

06 · Compounding

Lessons that compound

Every client run feeds back. The system gets better at your niche than any general agency ever can, because it only learns from your work.
